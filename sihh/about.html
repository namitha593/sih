Understanding Water Footprints

The water footprint of agricultural products measures the amount of freshwater used in their production. It includes three components:

  • Blue Water Footprint: Water from surface or groundwater resources used in production.
  • Green Water Footprint: Water from precipitation stored in the soil and used by plants.
  • Grey Water Footprint: Water needed to dilute pollutants to meet water quality standards.

Calculating the water footprint helps in understanding and managing water resources better, especially in agriculture.
